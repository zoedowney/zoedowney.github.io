imagery is the concern across my work, paired with the critical discourse that orbits it. presently my focus is on abstract details of still-life and subtler movements of street photography; scaling-down my approach and documenting the smaller—but significant—ordinary.

ongoing projects & features:

view my project 'shoe sighting' (@shoesighting), whereby I capture and curate photography that features footwear in beautiful or unusual ways. this work is inspired by my time at ual: london college of fashion where—in 2013—I spent a brief but experimental year studying to become a cordwainer.

read my contribution to pop sonality where I share my 10 tips for aspiring photographers.

a little more about me:

born in 1995, I graduated from goldsmiths, university of london in 2020 with my master's in american literature & culture. during this time I specialised in critical theory surrounding american and japanese animation. I earned my first-class bachelor's in english at goldsmiths in 2017.
